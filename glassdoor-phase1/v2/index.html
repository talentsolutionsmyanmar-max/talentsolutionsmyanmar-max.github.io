Two kinds of truth, kept separate

APPLYING ≠ WORKING THERE

Applying to a company and working at a company are different experiences. Evidence about each is collected, labelled, and shown separately — one never speaks for the other.

HIRING EXPERIENCE

  • Application acknowledged or silent
  • Interview respect and punctuality
  • Job and salary explained clearly
  • Any request for payment (warning signal)
  • Outcome communicated, time between stages
Contacted via ReferTRM P1-VERIFIED
What verification means: a real, timestamped contact/apply action happened. It proves an interaction occurred — it does not prove every statement is true, and it does not prove the person worked there.

WORKPLACE EXPERIENCE

  • Pay reliability, commission and deductions
  • Actual hours versus official schedule
  • Leave, family events, private correction
  • Management, respect, safety
  • Promotion path, targets in writing, job security
Contacted via ReferTRM P1-VERIFIED
Stronger claim, stricter rules: workplace statements need stronger evidence of an employment relationship (architecture dependency D-21). Until then, workplace narratives are held or aggregated, never published bare.
