01

Principles

Six laws. Every asset is checked against all six before it leaves the studio. An asset that fails one does not ship.

1 · Trust at a glance

The anti-scam signals — verified employer, honest salary, never pay a fee — are not fine print. They are a designed, locked component that appears on every job asset, in the same place, in the same words. Familiarity is the moat against fake recruiters.

2 · Worker as hero

The Mother Test: would a worker's mother feel proud, not pitied? No desperation imagery, no "escape poverty" tropes, no charity framing. Our people are professionals building livelihoods.

3 · Mobile-light, always

3G-first. Low-end Android first. Type stays legible at small sizes, contrast survives cheap panels, assets stay light. If it is heavy, it is wrong.

4 · Edge, not decoration

Sharp corners, disciplined spacing, glass used sparingly over dark. Nothing cute, nothing soft-focus. The product is trust; the design behaves like it.

5 · Two registers, never mixed

Youth-facing B2C may use the curated emoji set. Employer-facing B2B uses zero emoji. English primary, Myanmar secondary, always in separate blocks. Registers never blur.

6 · Honest numbers only

Every figure in an asset traces to a registered source query. No invented salaries, no unverifiable claims, no vanity metrics. If the source is not registered, the number does not appear.

05

The Trust System — anti-scam by design

This is the most important component in the system. Our content must be instantly distinguishable from the fake-recruiter scams the platform exists to defeat. The trust strip is locked: same words, same order, same placement, every job asset, every channel.

The locked trust strip

Verified employer  ·  Honest salary  ·  You never pay a fee

Trust green (#2FBF8F) is reserved for this system alone. The strip is never reworded, reordered, translated by the studio, recolored, or removed. CCO authors the official MM rendering once; it is then locked identically.

Anatomy of every job asset

  • 01Named, verified employer. Real company name, verification badge. Anonymous "a leading company" posts are banned — anonymity is the scammer's costume.
  • 02Honest salary range. Real figures in MMK, stated up front. "Attractive salary" is a scam phrase and never appears in our assets.
  • 03The no-fee line. "You never pay a fee" on every asset, without exception. Fees are the mechanism of every job scam in this market.
  • 04Official channels only. Applies route to the official domain, official handles, or the listing's own apply channel. Never to a personal DM.
  • 05No urgency pressure. No "only 2 seats left!", no countdowns, no fear. Real jobs do not need panic to fill.
  • 06Source attribution on board listings. Aggregated listings name their source, never imply the employer is a ReferTRM client, and route only to the listing's official apply channel.

Scam signals we never use

  • ×"DM to apply" / "inbox your CV"
  • ×Registration, training, or "slot booking" fees
  • ×Unnamed employers, stock office photos
  • ×Guaranteed income, "earn from your phone"
  • ×Countdown pressure, artificial scarcity
  • ×Salary hidden until contact

Bilingual Rules

English primary, Myanmar secondary — always. The two languages live in separate blocks with a visible boundary, never mixed within a sentence, a line, or a block. The studio ships EN masters with marked MM slots; CCO authors every Myanmar word.

EN — PRIMARY · 16px · FULL-WEIGHT COLOR Verified this week: 12 openings with salaries stated up front.
MM — SECONDARY · 14px · SECONDARY TONE · CCO-AUTHORED [ Myanmar rendering — CCO fills this slot. Dashed boundary marks the language separation. ]
CORRECT Full EN block above, full MM block below · Section-level language toggle on long-form surfaces · MM line-height ≥ 1.8 · MM render-tested on low-end Android.
WRONG Language mixing inside a sentence · MM smaller than 14px · MM as an afterthought in tiny grey text · studio-authored MM of any kind — including placeholders and transliteration.

Voice & Emoji Policy

Two audiences, two registers — and the boundary is absolute.

Youth-facing B2C — curated emoji allowed

Maximum 2 per asset, never in the headline, never inside the trust strip, never replacing information. The full allowed set — chosen for dignity and reliable rendering on low-end Android:

✅
📍
💰
🕘
🎓
🤝
💼
🎉

Anything outside this set requires a brand-lint exception. No faces showing distress, no praying hands, no fire/money-with-wings hype marks.

Employer-facing B2B — zero emoji

Not one. Not in posts, not in decks, not in outreach DMs drafted by the studio. Professional register carries credibility; emoji undercut it in this market's B2B context.

Zero-emoji register on every employer, client, and partner asset.

Voice in both registers: direct, warm, concrete. Short sentences. Real numbers. No hype adjectives ("amazing", "incredible opportunity"), no pity, no pressure.
